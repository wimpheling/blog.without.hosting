# Revision / Retrospective Blog Posts

Use this note when the user revisits an older published post or cluster of dated drafts and wants to update the idea without discarding it.

## Pattern

1. Read the original published article plus satellite drafts.
2. Identify what aged badly at the level of framing, vocabulary, implementation assumptions, and hype-cycle language.
3. Keep the durable problem separate from the dated substrate.
4. Let the user's correction reshape the thesis. If they push back on "this was hype", preserve the stronger version rather than arguing.
5. Draft a new piece as a revision, not just a cleanup: "what I wrote then, what changed, what still matters, what stack/architecture now seems adequate."

## Example: DAI revision

The useful correction was: crypto is not relevant only because DAOs were fashionable; it remains practically relevant because AI agents cannot open bank accounts. Autonomous software may need machine-native value transfer before legal/financial institutions know how to represent it.

Good modern framing:

- old article overfit to DAO/tokenomics/Holochain assumptions;
- core issue remains AI agency infrastructure;
- DAOs may become useful when there are real AI agents to govern;
- crypto may be an economic/custody primitive, not necessarily a speculative native token;
- the article should salvage good ideas: AI budget, work vs self-improvement, human contributors, forking/exit, open vs closed data/code, auditability;
- propose a layered stack: agent runtime, governance, economic layer, marketplace, identity/reputation, transparency/audit.

## Tone

Avoid generic dismissal like "blockchain hype aged badly" as the final take. The user's voice prefers sharper distinctions: what exactly was wrong, what exactly remains true, and what new constraint makes the old idea newly relevant.
